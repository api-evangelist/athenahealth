This article explores how clinician-led medical coding creates operational inefficiencies and financial strain. It identifies five burdens: staff inefficiencies, coding errors leading to claim denials, frequent regulatory updates, opportunity costs to patient care time, and clinician burnout, recommending separation of clinical documentation from coding-heavy administrative work.
